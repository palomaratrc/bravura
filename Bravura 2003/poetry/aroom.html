A Room I Love

I’m warm in blankets.
The blinds are drawn
Smells of her are filling my head
Except this spot on this pillow
Where I always lay my head,
that smells like…
Me,
She brings her diet Pepsi with her
And looks at me.
How long have I been asleep in her room?

I hold her tight in my arms and
look at the posters on the wall
all around the room.
Sun squeezes in
through slats in the blinds
and the dusty haze carries columns of light
towards our feet.
I don’t have to turn my head to tell she’s asleep
I tell her I love her
though she can’t hear me.
