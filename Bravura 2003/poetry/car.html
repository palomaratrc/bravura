Car

You:
inscrutable, irresistible, irrational, perfect.
Me:
paranoid, presumptuous, parasitic, insecure.
Your fingers play air guitar as you drive down the street.
It's dark, and I think you just ran a red light two blocks back.
However, I am not sure, because the blur was in my peripheral,
and I was too busy singing along.
My fingers rest on the edge of the seat, inches from the gear shift.
I always wait for you to shift gears before I say anything.
It seems safer that way: within the speed limit.
Sometimes I wonder what would happen if I just pulled back and forgot about you.
I don't want to.
It's not about what I want it to be about;
I hate it and I can't make you rhyme.
This is one of those silent rides
where time peels like old paint and smells worse.
You speed out of the turn;
I feel like we're rolling downhill.
I remember...
Last night we hung out in a parking lot, late and empty.
It was the best six hours of my year,
so far.
I fell asleep standing up, leaning against you
as we slow-danced to the stars and the traffic crashing like waves,
or my heart.
Because I don't know you.
It was too fast,
so I leaned over, whispered against your ear;
you stomped the brake pedal to the floor.
I think I got whiplash as you
pulled over and unlocked the doors.
But I got out and walked away on my own.
