A Conflict of Poetry

He sits with a myriad of weaponry,
Rhyme scheme, rhythm and meter.
His foe only himself, but his target
A the kingdom of false promises,
Placid white it lies there awaiting siege.
The poet moves his pieces into place.
The pencils and pens hold their positions.
Patience is needed for the right moment;
Too soon and the attack will prove vain,
Too late and the battle will be lost.

It’s time.

He moves in striking at the nemesis.
The defenses are strong and holding well,
The poet must remain the Captain of his words.
His ideas flourish and form into vivid imagery,
Catapulting his confidence to charge on.
There is no time for the adversary to retaliate.
Words fill the kingdom,
The once pure land has nothing left but retreat,
It takes its remaining forces to the borderlands.
The enemy has been defeated.
Though a battle has been won, the land is devastated.
Grammar all alone shouting for punctuation,
And casualties of words missing letters.
But the ideas are done fighting and it is time to rest.
The poet has proven himself worthy in battle,
for now.

 

Gated Tides

Do not listen to your father, he lies.
A child torn between two lovers fate.
Do not listen to your mother, she lies.

Child in the night hears his mother’s cries.
He tires to scream, but the screams come too late.
Do not listen to your father, he lies.

The father knows no more love, though he tries,
His attempted words will come out with hate.
Do not listen to your mother, she lies.

The end is near but the mother denies.
For the child, the mother will translate,
Do not listen to your father, he lies.

The parents absent, as childhood dies.
Long lasting words are his parent’s debate.
Do not listen to your mother, she lies.

When he is older he may become wise,
But forever will hear the words of eight,
Do not listen to your father, he lies.
Do not listen to your mother, she lies.

Line of Temptation

Waist Watchers frozen food,
Fat free milk and tofu,
Celery sticks and carrot bits,
All items in her plastic basket.
To the checkout line.
Ten items or less,
Should be express,
But the delay is great indeed.

Strategically placed are daily temptations:
Butterfingers and a Milky Way,
Bazooka Joe and Big League Chew,
And King Sized Snickers.
With her stomach rumbling,
And taste buds quivering,
One hand reaches for the evil,
A momentary hero speaks,
“Will this be all?”
A nod is all she can muster.
Temptation,
Neither in paper nor plastic,
Is still shelved and now beyond reach.

She heads toward the car,
Horror strikes.

“Shit!”

She forgot laundry detergent.
The devilry will get a second chance.

 

Long Endings

War.
It speaks not true.
Such a short word.
War is forever.
No after or before.

War.
It speaks not true.
Telling nothing of the memories.
Foretelling nothing of empty glory.
We are all children of war.

Peace.
It speaks not true.
Such an easy word to say.
Promising a rise that will never come.
A child’s dream conjured for hope.

Peace.
It speaks not true.
Does it lie between wars?
Have all the heroes gone when you return?
Hope not for peace in times of war.

Hope
It is the glory that moves us.
It is the hope for an end.
Peace will come.
When there are none left for war.

 

Shoe

My value was never really appreciated.
To the vain I was merely a statement of fashion.
The working class demanded more comfort,
And I when I failed,
Because I had to eventually fail,
I was tossed to the side;
Ignored like a vagabond.
The miles we shared are forgotten,
And you began a new search,
Looking for another to use,
And shoe your needs.
You will tread past me,
Everyday,
With this hussy,
Laces and leather
Were always what you wanted.
My tattered suede
And outdated lines are forgotten.
You break my soul,
And all I ever wanted was to support you.
Now my end,
65 ½ pounds of hair you named “Chompers”.
From this point,
I have two things to look forward to,
A torment of saliva,
And an eternity in an unmarked grave.
