Corners

I
There are times when the corners seem like the best place to be,
When the dark is covering me from head to feet.
Crouching in the rain of the moonlit trees,
hiding from the eyes that are searching for me.
Watching for a time,
the falling of the leaves,
and the desecration of my shallow body,
and my lonely tree.
Shivering in the rain of the fallen leaves,
from my stretching tree,
waiting for the time to creep through the light,
and go unnoticed into the corner that begs for me.
II
Winding though the darkness,
Now consumed in all its glory.
The tree that once was,
Basks bare now in dark light.
Empty of life.
Crunching its old leaves,
I walk through the lonely darkness,
Not fearing the watching eyes.
My corner left behind.
I walk in the spaces where the light used to be,
Begging for its return I fall and stumble,
To the worst place of all.
My dense dark corner,
with its view of my lifeless tree.
