Cloud Dancer

He danced the dance of rain, for the drought had brought much pain.
His moccasins circled the dust like drops of rainfall busting from the sky,
And soon he would let out a heartfelt cry.
He was Cloud Dancer, a spirit known well. He would dance his dance under the moon until he softly fell.
Suddenly:
A cry in the shadows, a raindrop upon a painted face. A gust of wind…
The spirit had danced.


Ceyena Grey Wolf

In the winter the grey wolf runs wild, as free as a bird as swift as a child.
He is truly alone without friend or foe, always moving and on the go.

His pack is behind him, ready and eager, patiently awaiting his next move.
It is diligence and power he has to prove.
He is always ready for the kill, using his cunning and mighty skill.

No-one defies him for he is the alpha leader. And all who understand him do not test his power. For they know whom to trust and obey… many who fear him look away.

Grey wolf, alone and wild, as determined as a child. He stands alone without fear,
Always knowing that his prey is near. His pack is soon by his side, ready to listen and abide. His endurance is endless and he has proudly earned his role.

If you listen closely, you can hear his call and let it touch far into your soul.
For this grey wolf is truly a spirit of loyalty, determination, fearlessness, and honor.

He is Ceyena Grey Wolf,

An infamous feeder,
And always,

“Alpha Leader”

 

Lakota Woman

She was a Lakota woman proud and strong
And she knew that she would always have a place to belong.
Her heritage was her own and her blood was pure;
For the Lakota woman had much to endure.
Her path was not always easy and her road not always smooth.
But her strength and courage always took hold.
Even during the white mans endless pursuit of yellow gold.

She bore children and made warriors out of boys,
Where bows, arrows and knives were their only toys.

The Lakota woman proud and true, honor, pride and war
Is all she knew.


Two Wild Horses

Two wild horses running free
In the meadow, along the tree
Running through the air,
With no worry or care,
Just running wild with his mare.

His hair flying softly through the wind,
With his only beloved friend.

Without a worry on his mind
Just looking for someone gentle and kind.

The Mighty Chief

It was a mighty chief they knew
For they knew no other,
For many considered him their blood brother.
He was old and wise, he told stories of long ago.
Of a place called Little Big Horn and storied of great buffalo.
He told of a time when many bellies were full.
He who acquired great honor was “Sitting Bull”.

His legend was strong, his strength he gained from the eagles and wolves.
Many hunters brought him great medicine and buffalo hooves.
He fought hard for his people, teaching them to be strong and brave,
Never allowing their spirits to rest in the grave.
He is honored by their belief, for he is and always will be..,
