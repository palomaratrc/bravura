How to Survive Being Mexican and White

A long brown braid.
A German nose.
A thin upper lip,
A fat lower one.

“What are you?”

Don’t answer that.
Or else you’ll have to explain
Everything.
Because one word
Can’t excuse

Why you love enchiladas,
But have never tried menudo.
Why you can’t dance cumbias,
Why you can’t roll your r’s.
Why you don’t drink tequila.
You must be a White girl.

Why your body turns brown in the summer,
But your hair goes blonde.
Why your best friend is a cousin.
Why you don’t date White boys.
Why you don’t laugh at
Field worker jokes.
You must be Mexican.

Go home.
Say hi to the Virgin on the mantle.
Kick back.
Listen to Johnny Mathis for awhile.
Think about your dead grandma,
Her apple pies.
Call your abuelita,
Ask her how to say
“I don’t know,”
In Spanish.

Enroll in a Chicano Studies class,
Pretend you know who Huitzilopochtli is.
Pronounce “maize” correctly.
Learn about the mestizo,
And write a poem about it.

Stop blaming
Mom for her whiteness
And Dad for his brownness.

Stop avoiding
Your boyfriend’s family,
Even though you can’t talk to them.

Don’t change you name
From Michelle to Marisol.

Ask your Tia for stories
About Mexico,
And your Uncle
About Black Lung Disease.

Inhale it all,
The menudo and the tripas,
The cornbread and the sauerkraut
Listen to the accent
Without translating in your head.
Dance stupidly.
Speak illiterately.
Nobody is watching.

Remember how to say,
“I don’t know.”

“No sé.”
