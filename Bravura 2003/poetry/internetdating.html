Internet Dating

She is not the vision I once beheld.
Either mine eyes deceive or is my wit,
This beast of brawn to me has been shelled.
She is more of a man than I am fit.
She draws close for a hug like a long friend.
I hear my back giving way to the bear,
No longer can I stand straight, I must bend.
Looking again, I know not which eye to stare.
She moves in for a kiss and I feel sick,
I point to the stars and attempt to flee.
She pulls me close and I feel a small prick,
Her belt buckle, I’m as sure as can be.
“To Dinner,” as she lifts me in her arms,
The last time I fall for internet charms.
