Jay Walking

I watch you from a safe distance
As you go about your daily routine
Wearing those leather pants that I love so much
You sit for a moment to fix your hair
Hair that I have imagined tangling between my fingers
As I whisper my confessions in your ear
You look into that myriad blue sky
Brown eyes shining with satisfaction, a smile playing on your lips
It’s a look I dreamed you would give me
If only I wasn’t so shy
I take a sip of my coffee
Fantasizing that the warmth of the cup was the touch of your hands
Reaching out for mine
When I look back up at you
You are sighing thoughtfully
What are you thinking about love?
I wish I knew what danced about your mind
That you would share your dreams with me
Have you noticed me before?
Have you seen me watching you with longing eyes?
You wet your lips now, a habit I have seen a dozen times before
(A habit that hasn’t exactly induced appropriate thoughts)
Do you know how that temps me?
How easily it makes me think I forget my shyness
And I feel I could just run to you
Pressing your lips against mine
The wind begins to pick up
Teasing your unbuttoned collar
Revealing the smooth, tan skin beneath
Beautiful
You can’t be real
And yet, you are here every morning
Getting ready to cross the same street
And I sit here in the coffee shop
Dreaming of a dozen things I could say to get you to look at me
“Excuse me sir ,but you dropped this”
“ I don’t mean to bother you, but do you have the time?”
I sigh as I rest my hand on my head
The light turns green
You cross that street
And I smile
Words lost forever between us
Oh well, there’s always tomorrow
