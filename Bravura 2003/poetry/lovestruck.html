Love Struck

Love comes suddenly, or so it’s thought,
You see the man across the way most sought.
And though you think life should be like a tale,
Thoughts of marriage turn him pale.

Ghostlike man, whom for which you seek,
Once so strong is now so meek.
The love you lusted for is gone,
After a week he must move on.

Utopia is lost as Atlantis is,
Looking for your treasure, you found his.
And now he wishes to stake his claim,
Dig down deep into your fame.

Snakelike he waits for the right time,
To commit his heinous crime.
Being a thief he’ll steal your heart,
Telling you he’ll never part.

But once beauty’s gone and youth has faded,
A fool to think he would have waited.
And so you’re left alone again,
Just you, some paper, and a pen.
