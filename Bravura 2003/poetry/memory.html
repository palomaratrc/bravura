Memory and the Sea

By the sea I reminisce old flames of dying light.
Together the collection bends and then wanes.
Small moments disappear and I am left colder,
afraid of what has gone.
It is the ceaseless pounding, the infringement upon memory’s retreat, which frightens me the most.
Where there was she and her luminescent ground of inspiration,
there is now only an echo.
Not of her, not of the times,
but of the same extinguishing waters approaching once again.
