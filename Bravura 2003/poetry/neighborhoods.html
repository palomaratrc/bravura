The Neighborhood's Expanse

This aint no Left Bank, so I got to
write about what's around me
The neighbor-kids are puttin about
In some old pickup truck and
Honkin the horn in short bursts of
Youthfullness

"Aint no one else to do it" says I
philosohically
So the housing development goes
In across the road, soon to be
Filled with people that would call
The cops when all the neighbor-kids
Are acting in such ways called
"Futility"

I once had a dream that the neighbor
girl called out "I love you!"
And even now I do not know if it
Was any more than my imagination,
But what if it wasn't?
For never did I call back "I shall
Make thee ivy garlands and beds
Of roses" and every thing else
Marlowe promised his little
Nymph

Seems I can fly to my literary world quite
more easily every day
And here I expand my mind,
Maybe more than it should be
Just because no one else
Would do it if they had the
Chance at it; and in my
Mind's vast expanse, I realise
That the time is still only
Sunday.
