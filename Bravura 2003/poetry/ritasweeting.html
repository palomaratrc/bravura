The sixth day


No longer running I hang my coats to dry,
Upon the fence of Jerusalem and sit my faith, then cry.
The blue has once more entered; my shutters still see the storm
I cannot bury sorrow and will no longer morn.

Just stand and wonder, who is my friend?

Adeste Fidel’s! Come hither!
Jacques Brel, Calais, Pierre Argil let,
Gerard Sekoto pray for me?
I miss your kiss known and unknown
This day, the sixth.
This time betwixed the lines.


The eighth day


When God chooses wisdom falls upon the soul like soft dew on the morning grass
Pearled and gleaming.

Suffering becomes a more bearable path; happiness is quiet contentment.
Love is not passion as much as thankfulness shared and joy is bursting strength,
Unbridled love for all.

On this eighth day I can see hope gleaming,
Brushing its hands ever so delicately across the bushes, blushing with its sunset.

The seventh day


The aftermath of betrayal is bleeding!
Reddish feelings descend my chest.
My breast heave heavy thoughts,
Laden with unforgivness.
This seventh day has no outlet.
We must live her as though she were
Our lover, our impossible companion,
Who has brought on a curse of doubts?
Accumulating again and again with each
Moment we remember.

How was she this day?
When God rested but man went on
To fight the Lord, and pierce my heart
With trouble and distress!

For Reason (a love poem)


There is Love
And there are Loves
Ours is of Loves
Forever winged
Forever above soaring.
Together or apart
Always one
Forever love.

Your distances but dance
My changes but cadence
In rhythm

Be there fault
It knows but truth
Be there tear
It speak but of reaching

And beyond deep
Passion
Nothing know better
Heart, ecstasy and
Tenderness found

My love
Our love is fixed
In the mosiaque of
The Great Crying Wall

Jerusalem be ours
For love is true.
