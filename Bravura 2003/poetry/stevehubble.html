Animal Parade

The swift flowing line of the animals dancing on high,
Above the lowest cloud and beyond the farthest path,
Caught the eye of the beloved opossum and her gang of rabbits.
They watched the gleeful spinning of the elephants,
And the rabid dance of the lily tiger.
Moving in and out of oncoming traffic,
They spun into a sea of out flowing mystery.
Spinning on edge to the highest riverbed,
Into the weary gushing waves of the lion’s nest.
Zebra’s joined the dance that caught the opossum’s eye,
And the lions trapped them all in their cave of death,
Save the opossum and her rabbit gang.

 

Insomnia


It sleeps inside us all,
the monster that creeps
into our dreams and
stalks our nightmares
without a single fear.
It chews away our minds
and gnaws upon our hearts.
forcing you to wake
in a cold sweat,
screaming for your life.
without you knowing
It’s keeping you awake
stealing your own sleep,
It waits…
It waits…
It waits…

 

Nefesh


Tiptoe through my garden,
do not tromp the fragile leaves
and harsh thorns of me.
Take each step lightly,
let the broken glass slip into your feet,
let it seep in and become a part of you
as it is part of me.
Reach into the rose bush
and pluck yourself a bud,
beware the bite of the gentle flower,
I warned you of its touch.
So soft, so sweet.
Smell the leaves
and feel your feet.
Wash your hands with the hose,
Wash your blood from the rose.
Clean your body, mind and soul,
and for a moment,
delve into my world.

 

Silent Ritual


She sits at the bottom of the hill.
You can see her between the stumps of
trees and the shadows of despair.
Perched upon a lawn chair vacantly staring
The blank shadows cover her shivering body,
and the stone in front of her
where her eyes are fixated.

Happy Birthday balloons whip through the wind
hitting each other in dead celebration.
The birthday wishes go unnoticed,
yet she still stares at the immovable stone.
Flowers wilt away but she never removes
her eyes from the stone
covering every inch in silent agony.

Tiny tears trickle from her eyes.
She does not bother to wipe them away.
She lets them roll down her cheek and
fall to the ground, screaming the whole way.
They splash on the same spot they did
years ago when she first sat here and cried alone.

Everyday she sits and stares.
She never speaks, and rarely moves.
Her eyes just stare in disbelief at the gray stone.
She watches the shadows
cover her stone, and then I am
forced to tell her to go.
