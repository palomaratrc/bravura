Withering Away

What do I see?
Well…it’s not what you see.
I didn’t think it would get this out of hand.
I still look fat!
I roar to myself,
as I pull my falling pants back up to my waist.
My skin is pale now.
My bones look like they are going
to tear through the flesh
and scatter across the floor.
I used to go to the beach
during those scorching summer days.
Eyeing boys with my crystal blue eyes
And tossing back long sun bleached tresses
That clung to my bronzed shoulders.
Now, I only hear the sounds of repulsion and fright,
as I drift by like a frail apparition.
If they only knew that I am frightened too.
My hair thinning,
Entangled in my hairbrush.
My eyes sunken into my face,
encircled by rings of dark shadows.
If I could,
I would smile,
and at least hide the pain from everyone;
but my transparent teeth
are only another thing to conceal from the world.
I look into the mirror,
but this isn’t me.
I’m dying here inside this withering flesh.
