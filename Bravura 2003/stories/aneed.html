A Need to Exist


     The need to exist was in the simplest of terms, non-existent. There was no need, no desire, not even the smallest hint of a will to live. But he did live. He still breathed. His heart still pumped blood to all the parts of his body. On occasion he still slept and ate. He lived. Time passed and he still lived. But he did not want to. He no longer wanted anything. And yet he had no choice but to exist in this world. A world that was indifferent to what he wanted.
     Dawn. Another day amongst trillions that had existed and would exist. The sun rising was no big surprise. It was the same routine: a mundane process without any significant changes. A reminder of the seemingly never-ending life that he was forced to live. The man, the memory of someone with a life, a job, a home, a family, and love, sat upon his high roof-top vantage point studying the burning ball of light as it rose slowly in the east. Far off mountains fought a losing battle to conceal the glowing giant as it climbed higher and higher. The man smiled at the mountains’ futile efforts. He had tried the same thing many times, wished with everything that another day would not begin, but of course always lost. On this morning the mountains and the horizon off in the distance lost once again. Another day had begun.
     The man smiled even deeper as a tear began rolling down his beaten face. Today was not just another day. Today was also the last day.
     He stood up, walked quickly to the nearby ledge of the high-rise building’s rooftop, and stepped over it. Below, the world suddenly grew a hundred fold. Not just because he was falling to it at a rapid speed. It was a strange effect he had not foreseen. Everything seemed bigger suddenly. Not just the streets below or the horizon in all directions, but everything. The world beyond what he could see was stretching, swelling past boundaries that had seemed so definite. Possibilities, absent only a second ago, arose from every angle. Hopes suddenly overcame rapidly diminishing fears. All matters of the past began surrendering to a born again future. He could love again, with time. He could rise again. Start over. He could follow his dreams and make new ones. It was all before him now. He could live. He could deny time. He could, but as the world drew ever closer, he realized he had made a mistake. A final mistake. He closed his eyes and screamed in terror. It was over three seconds later.
