Denying Z


Y- “I prefer a triangle. For you see, a circle simply goes round and round with neither low nor high points. Life just continues on and on without interruption. With a triangle, there are moments when things must change. Life suddenly takes a sharp definable turn. A triangle allows three such changes.
X- “Why only a triangle? A square allows four. A pentagon, five. A hexagon , six. And so on.”
Y- “With any other multi-sided shape you might experience more changes, but with so many angles, life again resembles a circle. A triangle allows but one climax, one moment to savior for its maximum value.”
X- “But with a triangle, do you not again end of up repeating yourself? Just like a circle?”
Y- “It is true that if you do not remain at the top, you may only move back down. And eventually you will arrive back to the same point you started at. But at least there are moments of change along the way. You rise, you fall, things level out, and then you rise again. And if you are enjoying a peak, you may even decide to stay for a while. What’s the rush to step down when life is high?”
X- “I see. There are certainly advantages to a triangle. I’m tired of moving in circles. It’s exhausting.”
Y- “Then let this be a climax and linger around. Stay for as long as you like.”
X- “I think I’ll stay here for a while.”
