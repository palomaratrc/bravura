Dialogue Story

     Nibbling on his top lip, Ben gazed at the glow in the dark stars on the ceiling. Georgina looked over at him and chuckled to herself, she knew he was deep inside his thoughts.
     “Whatcha thinking about?” she whispered in his ear.
     “Our future.”
     “What about it?” she pursed her lips as she waited for his answer.
     Ben grabbed her hands and closed his eyes. Georgina studied the perfectly centered freckle on his eyelid. Just looking at it spread a warm tingle all the way up her body and finally to her cheeks.
     He opened his eyes and locked them on her pupils. “I see you and me sitting on our front porch, watching our grandchildren play in the grass.”
     She closed her eyes and let his words wash over her face.
     “Every morning we walk into town for the newspaper and a fresh cup of coffee. After saying hello to a few of the locals we go home and make love on our hammock.”
     “It sounds wonderful, so when are you going to ask me?”
     “Ask you what?” he smirked.
     “Don’t play dumb with me Ben Granger, you know exactly what I’m talking about” the sharp tone in her voice made Ben smile.
     “What are you smiling about? You bring up our future more than I do, yet my finger is still bare. I don’t want to pressure you or anything but frankly I’m getting a little anxious.”
     “I will, I promise but you just have to let the moment come. I wanted to ask you the night we met but I didn’t want to scare you off.”
     “I would have said yes if you had.”
     Ben leaned in and kissed her velvety lips. He moved his strong hand towards her face and brushed away invisible hair strands. She could feel his flesh on her face even after he took his hand away.
     When he opened his eyes, she was biting the corner of her thumb. “What’s wrong?” His forehead crinkled as he looked at her.
     “I’m afraid to tell you.”
     “There’s nothing you can say that could make me leave you.” His heart started pumping faster and faster, as he spoke the words. He held his breath waiting for the worst. Her silence was killing him.
     She kept staring at the wall, pretending to be interested in a painting a friend had given to her.
     “Georgina…”
      “I’m pregnant,” she blurted out.
     Ben just stared at her, not knowing what to say. His mouth opened and then closed again, and he pushed all the air in his body out of his nose in an agonizing speed.
      “Say something, anything. Please I need you so bad right now, please.”
     “Are you sure?”
      “I’m two weeks late. I took the test yesterday. I should have told you, but I wanted to make sure first. What do you think?”
     “Well, …. I guess I’m a little shocked, as well as a little excited.”
     “Should we keep it?”
     “What do you think?"
     “I don’t know. What about school? I mean, it would be really difficult raising a child while going to school.” her chin quivered as she talked.
      “School is always going to be there and it’s hard either way. The important question is, do you want to have a baby right now?”
     “What is that supposed to mean? You don’t think I’d be a good mother?”
      “Of course I wasn’t implying that. I just meant that you are the one who has to carry it, and then give birth. Are your ready to do that?” he made sure to choose his words carefully so she wouldn’t get upset.
     “I need a few days to think about it. To be honest, the thought of being a mother makes my heart stop.”
     “Take as long as you need, I can wait. I love you so much Georgina, and I’m here for you.”
     “I love you too, Ben.”
     She turned off the lights and kissed him goodnight. It took her a while to fall asleep. She rolled from side to side desperately trying to get comfortable. Her head felt heavy tonight, it seemed like the fate of the world rested in her stomach. She tried thinking about school, family, work, anything that would take her mind off of the baby. Finally she fell asleep, but the dream world was unkind to her eyes. She heard a baby crying in the distance and then a chainsaw rumbled and blood was everywhere, she jolted awake and gasped for air.
     Ben sat up. “What, …what is it? Are you okay?”
     “Yeah, I just had a nightmare, go back to sleep.”
     She got up and went to the kitchen for a glass of water. As she stood their, she couldn’t shake the dream off. She splashed some cold water on her face and went back to bed.

     The next morning she left for school before Ben had woken. She needed to be away from him so she could think.
     What if she forgot to feed the baby, or didn’t play with it enough, or love it enough? Would it hate her for having it? She laughed at herself for thinking that she would forget to feed the baby, but what about the rest of it?
     She knew that Ben would raise this baby with her, and he would probably be a great father, but what bothered her more was would she be a good mother?
     She turned on the radio to drown out her thoughts. For the rest of the drive she sang along to whatever came out of the speakers.

     School went along smoothly, as if nothing had been said and she was still whole inside. As she drove home, her stomach started knotting again. She hoped that Ben wouldn’t ask her if she had made a decision because she didn’t think about it, or at least she tried not to. Walking towards her grey front door, she heard music coming from within. When she entered “At Last” was undulating out of the stereo, candles flickered around the room, and a bouquet of wild flowers delicately rested in a vase on the brown rectangular coffee table.
     “Ben, what is all this” her voice cracked.
     “Georgina, is that you?” he called back from the kitchen. She could hear pots and pans clinking together. Ben came out of the kitchen carrying a glass of white wine. He set the glass down and advanced over to her and took her backpack off her shoulders. He placed it on the floor and then took her hand and led her over to the couch. “Sit” he commanded, and walked back into the kitchen.
     Georgina’s shoulders lifted as she took a deep breath, and when she let it all out she felt lighter than when she had walked in.
     A few minutes later Ben came back into the room and sat down next to her. His right arm gently wrapped around her waist and his lips met her curled forehead; unable to hold back any longer Georgina let the tears come. Her body collapsed into his as a deck of cards would in a windstorm. She shook violently but made no sound, not even a lost muffle. Ben’s masculine grip did not budge the whole time. When she was done, she looked up at him and smiled.
      “Thanks, I really needed that” she sniffled.
      “I know honey. Do you feel better?”
     “A little bit. So what are you doing in the kitchen?”
     “Oh shit!” he jumped up and ran out of the room.
      “Did you forget about something?” she called after him.
     “No, everything is under control. You just relax and dinner will be ready in five minutes.” His voice was as calm and confident as a spring stream gliding through the forest. He had a knack at putting Georgina at ease with just the sound of his voice.
      She picked up her glass of wine and swirled it around using only her wrist, she raised the glass to her lips and took a drink. She could feel it slid down her throat and enter her stomach.
     Ben came back in holding two steaming plates filled with food. A trail of smoke glided into her nose, and as she inhaled her stomach screamed for more.
     “Wow, you really out did yourself tonight. What’s the special occasion?”
     “Nothing. I just thought you could use a nice diner. I got us a movie too, you wanna watch it now or after we finish eating?”
     “Let’s watch it now.”
      He rose from the couch and put the movie in. She opened her mouth and let a quick breath of air in. She couldn’t find the words for how she felt at this moment. Her body swelled with love and nothing else mattered.
