A Folger’s Decaffeinated Coffee® Brown Bag, Rice Cakes,
or
Something Much More Important

     I wore a brown paper bag. Not the kind one puts their groceries in or the kind a drunk my wrap his favorite crutch in. This brown paper bag was special. It was constructed meticulously of brown Foldger’s Decaffeinated Coffee® can wrappers held together with tape, staples, and raw courage. The brown paper bag was like no other that I had worn before. It was my finest creation and unbeknownst to me, it would be my darkest nightmare.
     I walked proudly through the streets of San Diego, displaying my handsome outfit to all who would look at me. Women whistled at me, men where envious, birds rested on my shoulders, and every creature on the streets wanted to know who it was that had created such a beautiful outfit.
     “Nice rice cakes buddy!” A fair skinned gentleman yelled at me with glee.
     “I sure do like rice!” Yelled another.
     I merely laughed in modesty and continued on my way. I had no time for such indulgences, for I was on my way to something much more important. So I gave my cheering crowd of admirers a thankful bow and then continued on my way to something much more important.
     It was not long into my travels when the weather began to take a turn for the worse. High above in the sky, darkened ran clouds had wrestled away the last remnants of a cowering sun, and would soon be raining their overfilled bladders down upon me. As I watched the treacherous weather getting ever more frightful, I realized I had forgotten to wear pants. Ordinarily that would not be a problem, but in these days of economic turmoil in the rice cake industry, I knew better than to get caught not wearing pants in a rainstorm. I did have my beautiful creation, my brown bag outfit, but that only went up to my neck. That was a problem. All of my pants went up well past my head, providing invaluable protection from the dangerous and often unpredictable forces of nature. And now I was lying in the middle of a street with little more than a brown bag to protect me.
     Unsure of why I was lying on a street, I immediately jumped up and ran for cover. Screaming bullets of water pounded into my head as I searched in vain for something to hide under. I hid under a tree, but they still found me. I crawled under a nearby car, but they still found me. I used a passing stranger as a human shield, but they still found me. I ran into a tall nearby skyscraper. That really hurt, and the hellish raindrops still found me. I ran down into the world famous San Diego Subway tunnel system, only to realize that such a thing did not exist. And again the rain found its target. It seemed that no matter where I ran, the rain continued to pound into my pantsless head. Such torture, all for the joy and freedom of wearing a brown paper bag.
     Eventually the rain did stop. My soaked head began the healing process, the rice cake industry continued to fall, and all returned to normal. But I had no time to relax. Quickly, I began skipping my way down the streets of San Diego once again. Since I had lost a lot of valuable time, I began hopping on just one foot. At this high speed I knew I’d quickly make it to my destination, or yours, or even to that of the hairless goat traveling next to me. Who would do such a horrible thing to a goat?
     I hopped for seconds that seemed like days and quickly found myself deeply out of breath. I had traveled no more than two feet and was completely exhausted somehow. I had forgotten just how heavy a wet paper bag could be. Years of swimming in brown bag outfits through the treacherous waters of Lake San Diego should have taught me. Old Man Winters drowning to death in his sleep, while covered in a montage of brown bags should have been enough. But no, once again my failing memory had put me in jeopardy. Or was it a brown bag?
     I pondered this as I lay immobile on the street. I rubbed my non-existent goatee, wrinkled an inquisitive brow, and struck all of the fine poses of a thinker. I realized it was true. My brown bag had nearly cost me my life and had contributed to the failing of the rice cake industry for some time now. How could I have been so blind? In a mad burst of energy I jumped up and began ripping my brown bag into hundreds of pieces (857 to be exact). I ripped my bag up until I was stark naked. It was embarrassing, but no more embarrassing than the ridiculous Foldger’s Decaffeinated Coffee® brown bag outfit I had been wearing only seconds earlier.
     Did the ensuing crowd around me laugh at me? Did they smile at my good qualities and frown at my debatably existing bad ones? Did they attempt to flee in horror through a subway system that did not exist? Was the hairless goat standing next me embarrassed? No to all four, plus one yes. Did they throw rice cakes at me? Yes, yes indeed. A hundred rice cakes flew in my direction. Plain, Butter flavored, Carmel flavored, Cheddar cheese flavored, Ivory Ranch flavored, it didn’t matter. The rice cakes stuck to my soaked body like glue. What mattered was that my naked bosom was quickly covered by a firm suit o’ rice cakes. I was back now and sporting the highest of fashions.
      “Nice brown bag buddy!” A fair-skinned gentleman yelled at me with glee.
     “I sure do like bags!” Another yelled.
     I merely laughed in modesty and continued on my way. I had no time for such indulgences, for I was on my way to something much more important. I gave my cheering crowd of admirers a thankful bow and then continued on my way to something much more important.
