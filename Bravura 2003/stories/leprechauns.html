Leprechauns, Tea Leaves, and Katherine Hepburn Naked

     I have a problem with leprechauns. The problem is serious enough that I am concerned about my life and yours. I think that I may have uncovered a truth that no man, woman, or mime should ever have to witness.
     Have you ever taken a bite out of a bologna sandwich and discovered that it was not bologna, but in fact tea leaves that the sandwich contained? Of course you have. We all have. Well, that has nothing to do with my leprechaun problem. Or does it? I did not think so the last time I ate a tea leaf sandwich. I soon discovered differently.
     As we all know, tea is the primary national product of French New Guinea. Whenever one eats tea, they eat a small piece of French New Guinea. French New Guinea tastes real good. A not so well known fact about French New Guinea is that a leprechaun population flourishes there. I discovered this fact accidentally one day when I overheard a conversation between two very loud employees at a McDonald’s drive-thru window. Through that informative ease-drop, I discovered that French New Guinea was overcrowded by leprechauns. So crowded in fact, that the leprechauns there fill every inch of land from coast-to-coast. That seemed harmless enough. After all, how could it possibly affect me? As long as I was wearing clothes and could continue eating tea leaves every day, I had no concern for French New Guinea's social problems. That lack of concern nearly got me killed.
     Not so long ago, during a Sunday afternoon in Balboa Park, I was happily miming a bologna sandwich when suddenly an angry clan of leprechauns attacked me. They took my money, my toupee, three small chairs, a kite, my libido, my laundry detergent, Katherine Hepburn, and my tea. It took me while to figure out exactly what they had taken from me. It was not until I stood naked at a McDonald’s drive-thru that I realized that my tea had been stolen. Seven weeks later I was told that my clothes had been taken too. But it was the tea that continued to echo through my mind. That was when it hit me. It was not my clothes that the little elves had been after, it was my tea. That shook me up so hard that on the way home I slipped off of the sidewalk and fell into a nearby ocean. As I drifted away from the coast I looked back just in time to see an angry leprechaun screaming "That tis' what it feels like to be a French New Guinean...arrgghhh!"
     An angry leprechaun indeed. My own selfishness had not allowed me to see what eating a simple tea leaf sandwich can do to a midget elf living on French New Guinea. Every bite one takes from a delicious tea leaf is bite of French New Guinea soil. With no more space for the leprechauns who live there, people like me had unwittingly been causing hundreds, perhaps thousands of leprechauns to fall off into the sea and drown. I had been a murderer and not known it.
     To make up for this terrible oversight, I have recently begun shipping boxes filled with soil and bologna to French New Guinea, in hopes of helping them reclaim their lost beaches. One might think such actions would mean an end to my leprechaun problems. I beg to differ kind reader. I can still feel hidden eyes watching me when I go to the park. I know they are out there and ready to take revenge the next time I let a tea leaf enter my mouth. I have to be careful and you should too.
     Do yourself a favor and make sure that the next bologna sandwich you eat is not actually a hand full of tea leaves. It can save you from horrors that you can not imagine and it just might save lives.
