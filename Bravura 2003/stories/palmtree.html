Mechanical Palm Tree

     Smog induced red gashes ripple across an ominously darkening gray sky. Through the haze, you see a vision, a peerless structural beauty, towering over its counterparts. You lust after this titan, and instinctively crawl towards the visage of omega organic perfection whose fingertips tickle the borders of the ethereal plane.
     You fall to your knees at the feet of this lumbering behemoth, hands outstretched, surely, just to touch such a godly specimen of agriculture would bring about a spiritual catharsis which enlightenment itself would be tantamount.
     In all your human impudence you touch this being, and in all your human fear you recoil. This is not the earth rooted natural Adonis you had suspected. Its bark can be husked to make no parchment, and its meat will fuel no fire bright.
     Your senses have been betrayed. The oil of shame and guilt drips heavily into the waters of rage. Truly this is the product of the spectacle of man, and not the beauty of nature. But has it not fulfilled your expectations? Have you not reached new spiritual plateaus? What lesson has your betrayal by this demonic pillar taught you? It speaks to you as you press your palms against its cold metallic exterior.
     It declares this:
     “There is no honor in being human. Surely the worst amongst the myriad of repulsive traits your species possesses is its seeming inability to acknowledge its own inherent repulsion. Your leaders wage wars, MURDER, in cold blood, and call it justice. Your cultures create superfluous waste which you can’t even stand to live amongst. You call your crudest obscenities entertainment and your finest art obscene.
     Instead of striving for spiritual elevation you induce chemical ignorance.
     “Perhaps the day will come when you will learn to accept your atrocities as part of your nature, then, and only then, in my long dead optimism, might I ever foresee your race overcoming its need to commit such atrocities. Until then, though, let me stand as a tribute to the sinful complacency of the species. As I stand erect, I am both tombstone and eulogy to the planet Earth.”
    You are confronted with complete ambivalence towards this Mechanical Palm Tree. The hate you feel proud in feeling, for surely this metallic structure disguised as an earthborn palm tree represents the zenith of human ugliness, yet the love you feel in spite of yourself. The love stems from the comfort and complacency that rests in the gut of all your species. This Mechanical Palm Tree, despite the hideousness it represents is a relaxing thing to look at, calming, and soothing to your heart, as opposed to the eyesore an actual utilitarian metallic structure stripped of its aesthetic pleasantness would surely be. The hate may make you feel unique, but the subconscious feelings of comfort, despite your greatest efforts to deny them, and despite their base nature, holds you in league with the rest of your species. You then understand that there is only one level which humanity can ever hope to reach understanding on: THE LOWEST COMMON DENOMINATOR.
