Waiting to Make a Move

       Roberto had not shaved this morning. He wondered just how scruffy that now 36-hour growth protruding from his face looked. Slowly, ever so slowly, he reached up to his face with a slightly shaking right hand. He touched the side of his cheek, felt the inevitable forest that was now growing there, and then began moving downward to his jaw. There it grew even thicker and coarser. There was no doubt in his mind that he looked pretty rough right now. He normally was a clean-shaven and respectable looking man. Hardly a morning ever went by that he did not repeat that process of making his face appear soft and youthful. Clean looking. It was a part of his life to look that way. Expensive three-piece suits, the briefcase, absolute sociality, were his life on any given day. Except today had been a day away from the routine. And now here he was at this time, this place, looking like a transient who had wandered in off the streets...
       What a shame.
        He looked across the space of the large room he was now in and made eye contact with another young man only a few years younger than himself in age. It was his first attempt to make some kind of contact with anyone else here and it was a success. More than a success. This young man appeared to be quite large, in a fit kind of way. He looked strong and powerful. Solid. At this moment in Roberto’s life that was what he needed. The soft gentle curves a woman or the fleeting strength of an elder gentleman nearby would not be very useful to him right now. Even the average man might lack what he needed unless he had a sudden rush of adrenaline racing through his blood. 
       The young man, tall, broad shoulders and powerful looking arms, had a shaved head which undoubtedly helped him look tough on any average day. But now the young man, his eyes locked on Roberto’s, showed that he was scared like a frightened child. That petrified look in the stranger’s eyes shook Roberto to his bones. A sudden rush of guilt raced through him for what he was thinking right now. He closed his eyes…
       He pictured Juan, so young and still new to the world. Innocent. His 13-year-old son, the young version of himself, would be wondering where he was today if he went through with this. Today was one of Roberto’s few days off of work. An actual day off unlike the mock ones that filled up the space of nearly all of Roberto’s so called free time. He had promised Juan that he would stop by his mother’s house to pick him up early in the afternoon and then the two of them would spend the rest of the day fishing off of the sandy shores of the Gulf. Juan loved those moments, Roberto knew this. He felt so much guilt over the time he never spent with his son. Especially now... What right do I have to do this?
       He had to. With his lips, Roberto formed a singular word. “Now”. The silent word carried over across the room to the scared younger man. The young man’s eyes grew large. His face filled with confusion. Roberto could not tell whether that clearly evident confusion was because the young man did not understand him or because he did not know what to do with that word. That thought. That choice.  Roberto repeated it again, much more boldly and clearly, and this time a faint whisper hugged the word as it formed on his lips. The young man’s eyes grew even larger. He began to shake so violently that Roberto could see it from his position almost twenty feet away. Slowly the man’s head turned from side to side, a clear “No” given back to Roberto. Roberto responded back with another “Now”, this time even more audible and obvious.
       A young and pretty woman next to him, dressed attractively in minimal business attire, “ssshhhed”  Roberto. Roberto ignored her and continued pressing the young man across the room. Ignore her as he tried, the sudden interruption by the woman into the two gentlemen’s silent communication did play with Roberto’s memory. His memory of soft skin, angel-like wavy blond hair, green eyes and long slender legs…
       Amber, that dream that could never be. How she had never fitted into his fast life. There had never been time, only fast commercials that allowed the smallest taste of love to fill their marriage. And then she was gone with Juan, and now, a new lover from what Juan had so accidentally leaked out recently. He had often dreamt that somehow there would be another place and another time where the two of them could be together again. But not now. Now his time for that was over. Now it was suddenly too late. Now….
        Now!
       Roberto knew that he had waited too long, but he made his move anyway. He suddenly began sprinting across the room with all of the strength he could muster in his reluctant legs.
       “Now!” he yelled.
       The young man across the room put his hands up wildly and turned to face two men standing next to him. Both of those men held a piece of metal and synthetic material tightly in their hands. One pressed the metal firmly against the young man’s head and then a flash erupted sending his brains scattered across the room, across other dead bodies lying on the floor with similar fatal injuries.
       Roberto suddenly stopped in his tracks just two feet away from the ski-masked men standing in front of him. A 45-caliber judgment of life and death pointed directly at him now.        Roberto then heard the loud crack of a pistol shoot off somewhere next to him and the attractive woman that had told him to “ssshhh” moments ago flew violently to the ground.
       He was shaking now, at the knees, at his waist, his whole body. He took one look around the lobby room of the First Union National Bank. Around him, a sea of motionless bodies lay on the floor, covered in blood. Other than the three well-armed men dressed in black and wearing ski masks, Roberto was the only one left standing alive. He closed his eyes and thought of Amber and Juan...
       I’m sorry I waited.      
       Their images resting inside his mind were the last he saw.
