I Tried
Hana Al-Iesa

     I couldn’t sleep last night.
     It happened again, this time in the corridor for all to see. I was sitting there in my apartment, jotting down a few notes from my text book and dreaming of what I could become. That is when I heard her, the nameless women from the upstairs apartment. nameless, and yet everyone seems to know her face. First I heard a loud thud against the thin wall of my apartment followed by a whimper of pain. I looked outside my window and saw something on the floor. I left my apartment and came around to where the object was. All around me it was stagnent, silent. I picked up the object, her purse and proceded to fill it with the scattered belongings. Some children were looking from their upstairs window, I asked if they dropped anything. “No.” I went back into the apartment and looked for identification. There was her drivers license, a happiness on her face that I had never seen. Along with it, his I.D. card, a straight face and eyes that seemed void of emotion.
     I stepped outside the door to take it to her and there she was. Mascara running down her cheek, hair all a mess. She was terrified. I asked if she wanted to come inside. “No, it’s okay, I’m fine”.
     He quickly came up behind her and hurried her up the stairs to their apartment. I heard a voice from behind me, “ Just come in the house, there’s nothing we can do.” Again I heard a thud immediately followed by a whimper. I ran up the stairs, “What the Hell do you think you’re doing?!” He looks at me with those vaccant eyes, blue eyes that would seem angelic were it not for the evil behind them. He was a small man, at the tallest 5’5” very thin. “No, no”, she cries, “ I just dropped something, I swear.” She is quick to defend him. What is this hold he has on her, Fear? Money? Could she actually love him? He doesn’t love her, that’s obvious. He just wants something he can control.
     “Let’s just go babe.” How could she call him babe after what he has done to her? She runs down the stairs. He tries to follow, and I step in front of him. In an attempt to hide his cowardice he looks towards me and says, “what’s up?” It was then I realized I was not alone as a voice comes up from behind, “You’re a dick!” We let him pass, and as he left we saw a scratch mark on the back of his neck. Her last attempt for an escape. Does he like to see her run in fear of him? Is it a joy to mark his property with a brusie? Something I will never understand.
     Back at my apartment I stand there and cry. I hear, “ This is how the world is, there’s nothing we can do.” There’s nothing we can do...
     I couldn’t sleep last night.
