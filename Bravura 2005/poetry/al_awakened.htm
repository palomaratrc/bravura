Awakened
Julie Alexander
Awakened by his own weeping,
cries only the shattered can make,
suffocated sadness released from within,
a soul full of endless ache.

The stillness of dawn beckons,
hints of sunlight reaching below,
betrayal of unconscious slumber,
a new day wanting to know.

Whisper of your loss and loneliness,
sense my tender call,
still embraced by lingering moonlight,
entrust me with it all.

Hesitant, he struggles for silence,
frightened by open despair.
Hands feel the heat of tear stained cheeks,
lips of salted cares.

Waves of anguish crash through him,
a sea of sorrow freed within,
soul bathed in healing sounds,
of water, wave and wind.

Intricate weave of acceptance sown,
As abiding moonlight slips away.
Translucent light on the ocean's edge,
dances into warmth of day.
