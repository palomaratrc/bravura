Torn
Brianna Baxter
Tired is the word I'd use
to describe the face I see.
You look up as some people pass,
then look over at me

Your eyes are dull yet very blue
and lower as you frown.
I feel your spirit torn and broken
dead and upside down.

Your hands are bound by fingers laced
and tremble as you think.
The table rocks unsteadily
that makes you spill your drink.

The floor creaks and you turn your head
then smile at a face.
Standing, you receive a hug
and relish the embrace.

Returning to the plastic chair,
you quickly clean your mess.
I notice that your face changes
to one of hopelessness.

Your eyes light up as friends pass by
then lose their sparkle fast.
I see that there's a light inside
but only from the past.

Your cuts are deep and hide inside
to make you not believe.
The clock ticks and tells you the time
you grab your bag to leave.

You don't see the footprints you left
inside the whole world’s chest.
My watchful gaze took in your mind
and learned from your distress.

When you sat down you called to me
and helped me see within.
I saw your torn and tattered soul
even behind your grin.
