Carnival
Jennifer Gottesman
If you take the hassle free shuttle
to the County Fair,
you get to ride in a yellow school bus
and enjoy a tour of the back lot
of the fair grounds,
where the carnival people live.
First, you'll pass
the Midway Badging trailer
where the workers get their IDs.
Then, there's the trailer where
the performers check in.
As you turn left at the corner
towards the employee entrance,
you'll pass rows and rows of RVs
lined up neatly against a chain link fence
like houses in a suburban tract.

A young girl playing
in her temporary yard
of artificial turf
under an awning
that pulls out from her home on wheels
makes me wonder
how she got mixed up
in this crazy life.
Maybe her Momma drives the Dooley
that pulls her house,
while her Daddy drives the rig
that pulls a ride like Winky the Whale
or The Vominator (for her sake,
I hope it's Winky the Whale).

With their family business
all folded up, neatly inside
a giant shoe box,
they parade up the Interstate
following the Fried Twinkie Wagon
and the Spiral French Fryer
to their next temporary home
where everything seems the same
where for her, everything is the same.
