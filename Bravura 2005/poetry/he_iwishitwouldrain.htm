I Wish It Would Rain
Natasha Heckendorn
There's a moth in the corner by the sink
being eaten by ants, one piece at a time.
I look at myself in the mirror
and I have to look away.
He was here not long ago,
smoking a joint and humming along with Blind Melon.
That was back when I could hear the rain on the roof
and my moth would fly about the window at night.
I still smell him in this room
and Blind Melon won't stop singing in my head
and the moth is still being devoured.
People come by to sit and talk,
unaware that a life is being ripped apart
into tiny pieces
just a few feet away.
