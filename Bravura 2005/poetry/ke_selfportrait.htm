Self Portrait
Elayne Ketcher
If I could paint a self-portrait,
I would be shown,
One body, several people inside me,
All very different, very complex.
There would be dark mysyerious colors,
Yet inside there would be much light
To show the brightness I feel in my Spirit.
And the painting would show me,
A woman, child, my body wrapped in yours.
My lips sucking you.
But your manhood has somehow been replaced.
I am sucking a needle, getting a fix
From your body.
I am addicted to you.
I am rebellious, yet I must submit to you.
I walk away, but always run back to you.
I crave you. Physically crave you.
When does the prey become the hunter?
The eyes tell the story.
As I suck the honey venom,
I may drink the very life force from you.
