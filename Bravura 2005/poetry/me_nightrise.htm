Night-Rise
Elizabeth Meilhaus
Dream-shaped kisses
of the velvety variety
wait upon the lips of the unknown.
Lashes rest on porcelain cheeks,
flutter restlessly.
Love and why,
peaches, cream,
sun and moon kiss the stars.
The grass is cold beneath my feet
as I dance under moonbeams
in my sleep.
Chocolate-covered dreams
drip with possibility,
rich to the taste.
Darkness blinds intuitions,
heightens the senses.
Roses cover the walkway of chance.
Step lightly.
