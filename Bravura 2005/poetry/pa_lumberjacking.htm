Lumberjacking
Douglas Panici
She was a vegan, pretty and wild
As any flower nature could ever compose.
I was a "Jack," meat eating and hardy
Brought up the way to split knot and acorn.

Into the forest, where the trees grow
Tall, the feral animals roam and
A flickering dim candle is not bright
Enough to have a flower take upon a root

But glow still, we shall and beware the calm
And the coolness of the hyena when one
Moves into the forest, for the lion knows
Of the hissing and the lying.

Only the end knows, the quick slip
Of a tongue is not yet what make trees
Grow or water flow or of what
Makes the sun shine no matter what

They show, it will never be what surely
Can be, of all that is meant to be.
From where my deer prance along
The way the river has laid way, and

Of top the tallest trees
Where the branch's tangle
The leafs scatter from Earth,
To whistle of a breath of air.

I would search the forest for the perfect flower
So I would know what it would mean
To have the taste of the sweet pollen
Be refreshed into every single breath I took.

What becomes present is all, which is factual,
The last of the spring flowers were picked
Perhaps never to ever grow again,
All that is left is dimmed dreams and a red

Empty vase. Returned to the forest, to chop
It all down, to leave a trail of stumps behind
Leaving everything untwined and finally saying
For once I can leave it all behind.
