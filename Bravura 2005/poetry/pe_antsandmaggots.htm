Ants & Maggots
George Pearson

Baby’s on all fours,
Surrounded by lovers.
Been preparing for death just being born:
Into hunger.
Telescopes on his loose knit rubber.
Bones crunch plastic,
Expanding through his skin textured elastic.
His health is the bi-product:
Of cork boards so escstatic.
Poked and stabbed,
Covered with postcards and pics, love letters and songs gone tabbed.
Like musical mathematics:
1 on the G
4 on the E
Loud and strong his fret boards ring.
Strummed and plucked, pushed and pulled too far,
As busted metal flings.
Down upon the dirty floor his casket.
Open (Of Course),
But only to:
ANTS & MAGGOTS.
