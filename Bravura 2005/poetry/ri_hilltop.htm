Hilltop
Travis Rivas

I went to watch a sunset
at a park
on a hill

Not for any poetic reason
but just because I had never seen
one before and wanted to know what
everyone was talking about
I sat and watched to the West as the last half
of the sun disappeared into nothing.

There were families there
playing frisbee and barbequing
Children sat in swings
twisted the chain around
to spin and make themselves dizzy.

I looked to the South
At the bottom of the hill
say my old high school
I had been to this spot
many times before
never to watch a sunset
only to look upon the past
always at night, always alone.

I turned to the East and was deaf in my ear
from the wind blowing to the South
I closed my eyes and listened
listened to the wind and twisting swings
I took a deep breath and wondered if this is what it’s like
the moment before you die
Do you stop for that last minute
and take in all that you never did before?
Or are you so preoccupied with dying
that you just let it all end with out taking note
I thought on this and turned

To the North to leave the hill
Fathers were disposing of evidence that
they were ever there
Mothers talked, making plans for tomorrow
Children staggered begged for five more minutes

I turned to walk away and saw a couple
who were facing the West the whole time
and had never said a word to each other
as I left all I could think of was how dry my hands were
