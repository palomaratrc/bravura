Exodus
Indiana Rogers

Hands folded while waking,
bridged and identical
Through theoretical space and time
New, revised environment
The scenery, the wilderness, the framed portraits
Attempting to endure

Familiar passage
Traversed, fierce, amended
Dearly departed, yet the mythology has been unearthed
A vacancy presented in the patriarchal hive
I returned
The causal cadence still a footnote
to my freedom
It was known at birth
But it came with conditions
Indebted to my makers, enlisted by my kind

Exodus of me, and my things
Our things
Idle matriarch
Passive observer
The eternal subject and pronoun
cherished and reviled
Divided heritage, severed branches of the organic symbol of origin
Earnest labors painfully unsung
All revealed throughout our odyssey
Forever nomads evolve into a new breed
But the generation
still goes undefined

The departure, not the escape
Bold, unforgivable, but classic dissent
Distanced the members, and the memories, of kin
Yet the life still travels
Remains visible
Like the long extinguished extraterrestrial
Still seen from space by the creatures that mapped it
Organized it, navigated by it
Though its historic existence had ended

The future becomes foreseeable
Though it is infinitely controversial and confused
I own it now
Genealogy traced
to the point of prehistoric oblivion
Legacies of lone descendents who travel on
They carry with them this knowledge
Home is a cleverly authored fiction
An evolutionary state of mind
