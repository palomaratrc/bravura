Betting on Toledo Street
Tristan Rust

Let me go down Toledo Street once again and
Join the suntan chorus of high school seniors
That pine for life at the edge of the seagull pier

The life that is served on the half-shell, blood rare
And grown only to be digested by the mincemeat
Adages of well-dressed wealthy gourmandes on Friday

A life built around bronfires dancing to dark low tide
And mmimicing the lookout kid waiting for the cops
To stream down the hill like a surfer’s imaginary wave

Let me cut across the jogging frenzy of the disheveled
Middle-aged that remain yards back from the
Lifeguard prowling: northt to south to evening till dusk

In pelican waiting on high-tide miracles I shall
Be beholden to the bikini inspiration of every man
Wishing steadily for a blonde-haired goodbye salutation

And where could you find me in the passed daybreak,
The churned seashells of rare late wavebreaks,
Too far to ever hear the parkingmeter sonnets quake?

Just let me bet on this perchance sunset bliss and stroll
Back up to Toledo Street till I reach white houses where
Once I slept without ever waiting on timelessness

Bet on the pea-gravel roofs and soot clamord chimenys,
Old men watering crabgrass lawns in overcast grey,
Old women inside cleaning the kitchens of family dinners

Bet on the sharp descent out of high-pitched dreiveways,
The leaving behind of childhood’s innocent hope skewed
By the shade of a queen palm tree grown up too fast

Bet on the one in particular day of the plum tree bounty,
Late night train horns clattering where waves are silent,
Cold plum tart midnights copping backdoor cigarettes

Let me bet on the tears that never flowed and the charcoal
Smoke of a California Christmas under vast sunsets knowing
That once a saviour came to remember the lost.
