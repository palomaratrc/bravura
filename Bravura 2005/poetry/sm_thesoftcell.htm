The Soft Cell
Kristian Smock
It's cold here.
They pump in icy air to remind you you're still alive.
The walls are sterile; they feed off time.
White ghosts haunt the halls,
cleaning waste and prodding skulls.
They watch you with vulture eyes.
Asking questions; telling lies.
They dig in your head with hollow words,
trying to gain your trust.
Oh, how absurd!
You're fed emotions with colored tablets,
designed to rid you of filthy habits.
If you exhibit a lack of control,
they'll give you restraint.
And this is perfectly legal in the eyes of the state.
They'll lock you in the soft cell;
the hardest place on earth.
To insulate with silence;
to isolate your mirth.
You may scream and shout out all your sins.
But no voices escape;
they stay within.
