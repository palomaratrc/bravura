Until Spring
Jesse Williams
It's a kind April morning
Glancing out my kitchen window, I sip my coffee
Strobes of light peer down from the trees
Illuminating my thoughts

I feel the warmth as the sun reflects off the dew on the new leaves
A gentle breeze makes them shimmer like diamonds
Spring has aroused life again

Walking out my front door I bend to reach for the newspaper
Smoke curls from nearby chimneys
Those inside stir from their temporary slumber
A car speeds by almost unnoticed

Now back inside, looking across the room, I remember you leaving,
Step by step, away from the light
My soul revisits another day
When winter's despair nearly took my life

     I suppose the first to go
     Was the love-bridge
     We labored so hard to build
     As you withdrew your arms from mine

Joy and contentment
Followed close behind
Compassion
And commitment left motionless
Neatly folded on our bed

In the hallway
Kisses and embraces
Like empty beer bottles after a party
Stand emotionless and silent-

Intimacy
Vulnerability
Strewn
A deck of cards flung haplessly across the un-swept kitchen floor
To be gathered and placed back into the delicate box

Conversation and laughter stand voiceless
Near the broken umbrella
At the open front door

Desire
Aspiration
Fallen
As you disappear down the lonely walkway leading to your car
Lay still like lifeless twigs

Dreams shared
Hope inspired
Precede her, huddling at the car door
You hurry toward them

Lesser dreams and promises left behind
Litter the yard
Rooftop, watching
Too devastated
To move forward

In a single motion
Both cruelly slow and callously fast
The car door opens
Beauty and the orphaned passengers board

My last memory
Soft legs, warm and encompassing
Disappear
Behind the metal skin
That will steal her from me

You drive away
Grace, mercy and love
Stare out
The distorted rear window
Incapable
Of gesturing goodbye

The unlit sky seems
To moan
To cry
A language indescribable
A blanket of snow
Subdues my exposed heart
Stalking my mind

My whole universe is
Lifeless
Grey
Thoroughly indifferent
An abandoned mine
Deplete of former glory

Remote memories
Unable to sustain life
Lament and whisper
In secret places of my soul

Love
Life
Lie desolate
Scattered like fallen autumn leaves
Naked trees
Standing frozen
Barren, without light
Striving for purpose
Existence
Until spring
