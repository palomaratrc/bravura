“10th Street Bar”
Emily Gravlin


Fiftyish woman,
Dyed red hair with gray roots—
Black-ringed eyes and blue cigarette smoke
Booth eight behind the pool table
Rubbing the green plastic polyester next to her
Empty ring finger with a thin tan line.

Old Bill at the bar
Always here
Counts grimy change from a pocket
On a stiff old Salvation Army flannel shirt—
Steals shots when the owner goes in the back.

Waitress with a sticky plastic tray
Caked red lipstick
Sings at 10:30 from a makeshift stage.
Barbra Streisand with a honeyed drawl.

Six-year old boy in front of the jukebox
Watches mommy fall off her barstool
Laughing.
His stomach grumbles
As he sips a Shirley Temple.

Owner and barkeep—
Cherry cheeks puff out steamy breaths
Onto spotty tumblers
He rubs with the old red rag
He keeps next to the till.
