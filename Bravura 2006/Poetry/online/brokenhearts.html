Broken Hearts and Rusty Nails
By Donna Booher


If you step on a rusty nail,
Scream. It’s okay to let everyone know it happened.
Try limping. If that doesn’t work. That’s okay too.
Let someone lend you a shoulder.
Clean the wound. It’ll sting some.
But that’s to be expected.
Let it mend and then,
Watch out for rusty nails,
Only to hit your head, instead.
‘Cus, you see, accidents will happen.
No matter how careful you aim to be.
And it’s hard to play like it doesn’t hurt much.
When it does. So let it.
Don’t pretend it ain’t nothin.
Because, you know, it’ll never heal right.
If you don’t let it sting proper
