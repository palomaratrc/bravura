Crows
By Clayton Beach


The crows are becoming a nuisance they say,
10,000 and growing in greater San Diego.
Crowding the morning mists with a “Cah! Cah! Cah!”
they flood the grenadine skies
in droves at dawn and dusk,
commuting to and from the local hotspots,
feasting on the trash that we leave behind.
One day, I nearly ran down a crow
as he hovered above a gory mess of red and yellow
already flattened to the asphalt.
At the last possible second
he abandoned his prize, greedily stealing
one last slice of pepperoni pizza, and then
he flew away, high above the midday traffic
with that slice of pie hanging from his beak.
Why blame the crows for adapting so well?
Why hate them for thriving on our chaos?
The gnatcatcher, his time is up, I fear,
for he cannot live amid concrete seas
and forests of false-pine cellular towers,
so learn to praise the crow and his brethren,
coyote and pigeon, for they have learned
to feed from garbage cans and highways,
don’t blame the symptoms of your own virulence.
