The Lake at Dawn
By Clayton Beach


The fog hung high
over the lake and
meandered to the glassy surface
in long, lazy fingers.
She imagined
that her father had taken her
on an expedition
into unknown territories
rather than just
some boring fishing trip.

The only sounds
were her father’s steady breath,
the ripple of water
purling at the kayak’s bow
and the slight splash
of his blade cutting water.
She had never before
witnessed such natural silence
and it enveloped her peacefully
along with the chill, moist air.

All of a sudden,
a patch of land loomed
out of the fog
like some prehistoric beast.
It was furry and green
with moss and bushes and
it stuck its long neck
far out into the silvery water.
A shadow flew above them,
and the wisps of mist
transformed it
into a giant, flying reptile.

Her father remained silent
as he rowed, and she
took in the scene
with deep, thirsty breaths,
drinking the feeling
of cool, misty air,
chromed, rippling water,
and white, wispy fog.
