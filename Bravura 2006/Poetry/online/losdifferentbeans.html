Los Different Beans
By America B. Feldman


There are different beans,
No solo los negros frijoles,
También los hay pintos y bayos
In tortilla or bread,
With guacamole and rice
In to the chicken dish or
carne asada and fish combo side
There are the very black beans,
        the charros and criollos,
There are any apparent differences
between the beans, but after a cook,
Cuban taste, Peruvian garlic or Venezuelan
in the boiling sight, and looking closely
        ….they are never the same.

In separated roads, in separated pans,
Many usually say:
“I never eat frijoles”
‘cause’ I afraid to forget my inglés…
“I never try birria con chile
only my fried bananas and mi roasted beef
        o mis arepas, pero nunca los taco…
“I not eat any salsa picante
I don’t like any hot,
        only my sweet tomatoes in’ cream…
“I never tasted enchiladas, tamales o mole
until I met my Mexican pals…
they are so nice, but I prefer
        my own ‘paisanos’ and land”

Then everyone point ‘las diferencias’
        between the beans…
But after a cool ‘reventon’
when everybody drinks,
the freezing cervezas con sal y limon
        all the frijoles together are in a happy ‘fieston’
Nobody stops, or anyone drops,
but they yell in the boiling saucepan…
y suelen decir para animar …
        “Echale candela mi negra”

While the beans are in the plate,
with different dishes and a flamboyant
        and exquisite ‘sazón’ and recipes
Nobody cares who brings
el Tequila o el Agua ardiente,
‘cause’ the important is the bliss in the stove,
Then the black round friends look almost the same…
Anyone doesn’t want to quell dancing and laughing,
Seasoning themselves in onion, garlic and herbs
        ….enjoying the loudly ‘fiestón’ in the vat.

There are the beans sharing hot water pots,
Walking, jumping in a crazy fray in casserole
Repeating the notes of the oil in their music…
but in one moment
they see each other in different way…
blighting the delirious dish
someone suele decir:
“I’m from Colombia, not from
la Patagonia or la tierra Azteca del sol…
“I am a mixteca, nor a quetzal,
neither chileno, boricua, tico or venezuelan…
Then adding coals to the fancy bonfire,
Others fold in the pan:
“Yo leo a Neruda
dream with Quixote,
instead of watching the ‘soccer’…
“I hear only Beethoven or Beach
never mariachis or bandas,
“I’m not brownie, I'm brunette
and my carnales all are ‘güeritos’,

“Cause in my blood runs the real blonde,
clear water as the skin milk”

There are the beans at lunch or dinner,
fighting for the white ‘herencia’
experiencing dissimilar worlds,
springing into unlike languages,
        but always dancing and yelling aloud…
While in ‘la cacerola’
In a very hot sauce,
There are the mixed frijoles,
        los bayos, pintos, negros o charros,
From the far land, or close to the gringos,
With different colors, dishes, and tastes…
but at the end all are the same
Frijoles son…and always will be…
        ‘los’ different beans of the place.
