On Turning 60
By Karen Wooton


Wow—What a surprise.
At 25 I knew I only had 10 years left, I knew I wouldn’t
        Make it past 35.
By 35 I had a child and knew I had to stick around
        A while longer.
By 50 I was beginning to hit my stride and my child was
        Nearly up and gone…and I was catching on.
At 60 feels like I’ve arrived. All those things in life requiring dues
        They’ve been paid.
I’ve got the hang of this life business now.
It took me every one of those years to figure some of this stuff out
        But here is what I know today.

The prime directive of life is just have fun
        Boredom is a choice—
        There are way too many wonderous things to engage me
        there is nowhere near enough time in a day to fit it all in.

The only things in life I can control:
        How I think and
        How I feel
The rest is out of my hands
        And most of it is none of my business.

The universe spills abundance into my hands everyday
        All I have to do is keep my hands cupped.

I have no control over the first thought that enters my head
        And some of the are duzzies.
        But I have total control over the second thought.
        I can change my mind at any time—I only have to want to.

All of life’s challenges offer a seed of opportunity.
Walking through a challenge with grace is finding the seed
        And saying ‘Thank You.’

Many individuals are highly evolved souls. Mankind is primitive.

My best ideas always require a second opinion.
I can dazzle myself with my own brilliance
To the exclusion of common sense.

As for how much older I get to become…
I only have this moment, maybe this day.
I’m going to live it knowing that if it is my last
It was terrific.
