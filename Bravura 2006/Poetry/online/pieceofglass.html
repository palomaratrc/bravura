Piece of Glass
by Sarah O’Nan


Your facade as a wall has been broken.
The mortar was play-doh and the bricks were all plastic.
And as it turns out, you're glass.
A simple shard of crystal.
Grasping at the covers and excuses
You stutter your way out of my sight
Step by step you back away
For fear you might get shattered.
I won't break you-
I can't break you!
You're too pretty.
Too fragile.
And if you're angled
In just the right way
I can see my own reflection.
