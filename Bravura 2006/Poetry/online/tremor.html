Tremor
by Sarah O’Nan


A slip of the tongue
No wait, the hand,
Repudiates my certainty.
Confidential movements
Whispering around my thighs
Prickling the flesh
And raising doubts
As well as goosebumps.
Sliding up and over
The hips God has given
For childbearing
Making indentations
In the soft flesh
With careful fingers.
East now, towards the navel;
Forbidden and mysterious,
Encircling without trespass
And down.
Silkily smooth and cautious
Below the glint of the silver button
And underneath the fabric
Pausing for confirmation
Before pressing onward.
Tenderly I grasp a wrist
Retrieving it before certain...
Limitations are stretched
Beyond reparation.
Why?
Because I said so.
