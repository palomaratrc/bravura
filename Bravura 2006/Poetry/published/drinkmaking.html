Drink Making at Starbucks
Emily Gravlin


Buzzing around
In their matching black-striped uniforms
(Even when they wear color, it melts into the background
and leaves them monochromatic)
The cluster around the bar
A hive of bees
Wings twitching
Abdomens humming with communication
Talking into cell phones.
They squeeze their cardboard cups
Branding their palms with the tattoo of
Modern corporate America.
A female WASP with Botox-tight eyes
Orders a tall, nonfat, suger-free vanilla latte.
A heavy, mustached Latino man in boots
And the tanned Abercrombie girl behind him
Each order venti, extra-caramel, caramel frappuccinos
And everyone pays with a five.
The rest goes in the tip jar
Which I get at the end of the week
To support my mocha habit.
