Elegy for My Left Breast
By Jenne A. Mendenhall


Farewell mend, companion flesh,
chesty childish, short lived girlish bloom
Too soon swaggering to woman's profile D
Nippled and primed for mezzo passions
in art and life.

Beneath your diva's prow, breath lifted
heart and ribs, happy singing
Handel arias, Mozart Brahm's songs
and Negro spirituals.

Flesh remembers pleasure, tired heads
bidden and unbidden, resting 'gainst
Roving infant hands, fervent mouths
loves fierce dark sweetness.

Gone now, old hurts and losses
disappointments long forgotten
But logged still in your mutinous ducts
as cancers on the move.

Farewell dear part of me
not two thoughts honored in our life
But cherished these last days
before the surgeon makes clean breast of thee.
