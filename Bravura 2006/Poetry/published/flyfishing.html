Fly Fishing from a Ferris Wheel
By Nicole Lee


The only girl on the ferris wheel
holds a can of unopened chicken.
She will get hungry later.
Her pa stuck a can opener in his
back pocket cause then she'll need
him, cause then she'll seek him.

A woman works the levers and sweats
a shiny lot in the unkind heat,
dark spots on her dirty overalls.
Dry brown grass, stiff in its death,
irritates her bare feet. She scratches
them on the backs of her calves, one
at a time, a slow-motion hop, something
of a dance, as the wheel throws each car
forward and pulls it back again,
unapologetically. She lets it spin too
many times too too
many times too
many times.

The girl's eyes close and she
imagines herself bait, a hook
through her middle, some large
fisherman throwing her far out-
first far forward,
and then down to the waterline-
the natural arc of a fishline casting.

Her pa has weight
like water-you can't breathe
when you can't break surface.

Her pa has weight like
suffocation that fills her
lungs while she helplessly sees that
no matter how much air surrounds
her it's what's in her, what's filled her,
that means she
can't breathe.

can't the girl can't and can't
and the girl can't do the can't anymore
there are no more tears to fall
like sweatdrops.

the ferris wheel casts her out
and then pulls her back too
many times too too
many times too
many times
pulls her back
flicking the surface

her pa sits at a nearby picnic table
he watches her get closer
and then pull away he watches
her get closer
and then
                  pull
            away
      he
            wants to
                  clutch her
            back to him
young girl fly

she knows for whom
            she is bait:
both the water and
the monster in it

a woman works the levers and
she notices for the first time
the skinny girl on the ferris wheel
the grass still irritates her
but she stops hopping
lifts her hand to her eyes and
follows the transparent fishline from
the girl's torn gut to the
man waiting at the table
for his next meal.

A woman works the levers and
she works the ferris wheel
pulling the girl back too
too many times
not enough not not
enough not
enough.
