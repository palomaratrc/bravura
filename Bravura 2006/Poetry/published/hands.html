Hands
By Ernest Lucero


I can remember
Years ago, as a teen
Looking at someone’s hands.
I do not remember who he was.
I remember only his hands.

I remember thinking how they looked—
Tired, scarred, dark from the sun,
Wrinkled from years of labor.

I looked at mine, smooth and clean, and
Remember thinking, How did his hands
Get like that?

Now, many years later,
I look at mine,
And I know.
