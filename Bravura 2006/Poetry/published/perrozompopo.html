Perro Zompopo
By Denise Brown


At 2:14 in the insomniac night
of Puerto Escondido,
explosion rocks the world outside
these walls, and sets me on my feet
running to the door, out to the porch
and scanning the horizon ‘til
my vision snags on bolt of lightning
in the eastern sky.
And CRACK! It fells me like a timber to
the hammock slung across the porch,
where, snagged again, I
see shooting stars above -
Perseid sparks like fireworks
in the steamy August night,
and rhythmic roar of tumbling Pacific
fills the black space in between
the meteors and lightning bolts.

A gecko chirps.
I know his Spanish name.
It’s etched across my brain electric blue,
and musty red Oaxacan dust
rises from the earth like dawn.

Alive in every pore,
I sleep.
