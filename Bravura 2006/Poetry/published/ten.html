Ten a.m. Sunday
By Linda Roux


As you slake your eyes on her in bed
she reminds you of an anaconda
curled among the amethyst petals
of a jacaranda tree. One muscle
of hers wakes the next until
you see she’s long as a schoolbus, but
much quieter. Don’t mind the panther
with malachite eyes, whose spots are
(then aren’t) the shadows of leaves;
or the sapphire-throated hummingbird
dipping into that moss-cool pond;
the long one will wrap herself around you,
tighten after each breath escapes,
help you to your own little death.
