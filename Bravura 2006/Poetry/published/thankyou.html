“Thank You for Flying Abstraction Airlines”
Third Place Winner, Poetry
by Elizabeth Webbe


Ambition
Curled hair and lipstick
(“I’m Really Not a Waitress Red”),
new pumps,
navy blue
to match her uniform.
Bronze wings for a 19 year old girl
flying at 35,000 feet.
Someday they’ll be made of gold.

Injustice
Paying $1500 for a
first class upgrade
to sit next to the man
who needs a seat belt extender.

Poverty
Skyharbor in Phoenix,
two stops left till home and
three days to payday.
Route 66 is the designated smoking area.
Eight dollars for a Bloody Mary.
Just enough for a double.

Loneliness
Feet hang out of the window
of a hotel room by the airport.
Another RJ900 comes in for a landing.
Rolled up nylons
A freshly pressed uniform
Pedicured toes
And an empty bottle of cabernet.
Smoking my last cigarette,
I play “Godspeed” one more time.
I’ll be home soon.

Contentment
A window seat on a Dash-8,
redeye half empty,
an extra pillow,
fresh out of the plastic,
lights out at 15,000 feet.
Dreaming.
