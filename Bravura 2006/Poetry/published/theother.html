The Other 2%, Conditionally
Second Place Winner, Poetry
By Nicole Lee


I will not be quiet when you touch me.

I will not whisper and simper like a reed
instrument persuaded to breathe delicate notes.
You are not a musician, and I am not a song.

I may cry

I will moan

if you touch me.

If you touch me I will not lie motionless
like Annabelle Lee,
I will not lie receptive like a depository
or doll.

If you touch me it will be because
underneath the petals skin
rises to the dew.
If you touch me it will be because
you've convinced dead petals
to fall—my
grey robe to slip—
disconnected,
down revealing a
dark diamond and
inches and fingertips of
clean bare lightness.

I will stand in the center, a bud
dropped in the middle of rippling silk;
then we will move together.

If you touch me then touch is
desired:
I want your hands
on me
because I want you
round me with me in me.

If you touch me,
let your touch be
gentle force
kissed and sweetly ripened.
Invited.
