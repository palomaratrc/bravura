View from the Back Seat
Wesley Heid


We were driving down a pockmarked street
through some broken part of town.
Looked like it had been through a war—
houses without windows,
boards covering their empty eyes.
Building walls
had lost most of their plaster,
the brick underneath exposed,
naked and dirty.
I saw a girl out of the corner of my eye,
sitting by the street,
her pink dress falling apart,
no shoes on her feet.
Dirty face,
dark, dead eyes.
I felt something in my chest—
guilt or something that I’ll never explore—
doesn’t matter, really.
In the end,
no one will notice when she’s gone.
