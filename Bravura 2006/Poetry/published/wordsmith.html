Wordsmith
By Karen Wooton


The wordsmith tosses words into disarray.
        No discipline.
The framework falls loosely on the page.
And then the work begins:
His pen, a brush,
        with sweeping strokes
                washes out a line,
                        overlays a word.
        Discards that turgid little phrase,
                paints nuance in its place.
        Eschews grandiloquence
                and seeks
                the simple phrase.
        Dapples bright-hued words,
        both crisp and stark
        Until stroke by stroke the theme unfolds,
Yet contrasts define the form.
        Shadowlands seek subtle words;
                the pen must find a softer shade.
The wordsmith suggests the pain
        and evokes the past in metaphor,
He tells a universal tale
        that whispers aloud,
                “I’ve been there.”
All of art is in the choosing.
The wordsmith knows the craft.
        Select each word with keen precision:
                Not “simile”,
                        rather, “metaphor;”
                not “hidden places”,
                        perhaps, “shadowlands.”
        Truth and only truth allowed.
At last the pen is set aside.
The wordsmith sighs and knows
        this little canvas has come to life
        without one extra word.
