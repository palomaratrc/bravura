A Confession of My Own
by Sarah O’Nan


I've begun to hate
your deluded narcotic confessions.
How you poured your innards out in front of me
unsolicited but by my listening ear
and prostrated yourself,
raw and exposed to my youthful eyes,
begging love and kindness from my undeveloped heart.
Now your blood has splashed onto my clothes
and I must fight the urge to turn away and retch
at the sight of your simple humanity.
I cannot still the downward strike
of the knife you wield against yourself
through my forgiveness,
nor my condemnation.
I will not be your Jesus
and I refuse to sacrifice myself on your behalf.
I much preferred the cool and smooth plastic mask
of light discourse and breezy fun.
The bright and smiling expression of which
distracted from your dark and frightened eyes.
I felt free, then, to go about my business
and leave you to yours.
With these secrets you've revealed
you grab me from behind and tightly clasp me to your chest
ignoring my blank stare
and unable to know my selfish dreams of freedom.
Squeeze out all the peace and comfort you can
from my stiff and ungiving self
before I rip through your cellophane dream
and politely tell you to
please shut the gate as you leave-
and don't forget the pieces of yourself
you scattered at my feet.
