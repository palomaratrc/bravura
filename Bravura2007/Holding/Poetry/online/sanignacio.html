San Ignacio Lagoon, March 2003
By Jenne A. Mendenhall


A green flash drops the sliver sun into the lagoon
Night, black and starless settles over the beach
Where we sip cocktails, ears open for whale talk, whale
Play squeaks, whistles, grunts, moans croaks
Water slapping love songs carried in by tidal wash.
Salt mists born of leviathan leaps spray us
Blind watchers of orgasmic rites
Rapt listeners to Ignacian water music

By day we lubbers dinghy out in deep green water
To see and touch twelve ton babies and their dams
Who glide under and around us in close formation.
They dive, emerge, approach to take our measure
Then standing on his tail a big eyed baby rides up
To stare us down, arms length away.
(For him no inkling memory of murderous men
Stripping his kind of ambergris and oil.)
We are beside ourselves, falling over one another rocking
Our boat to stretch, to touch the cool rubbery skin to stare
Back into the great unblinking eve.
