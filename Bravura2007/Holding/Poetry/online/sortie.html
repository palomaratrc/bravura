SORTIE
By Bill Bailey


He is top of his class, America's finest,
He sits in silence traveling toward
Hell with the speed of thunder.
Adrenalin flows, heart pounds,
The moment is now.
The sky is lit, reminiscent of some
dimly remembered patriotic display.
Deadly flashes blossom.
White and red and yellow, streak the darkness.
He flies on, into a kaleidoscope of peril
His panel and Heads-Up pour data into his brain.
He is one with his machine. A great calm envelops him.
This is what he was trained for. Find the target!
Aim the laser! Fire
Down the air shaft of military headquarters.
Orange fire bursts from a hundred windows.
A perfect shot and no collateral damage.
The clutching claws of anxiety return.
Avionic senses feel missiles reaching to destroy him.
By the book, he starts evasive maneuvers.
A comrade blinds his adversary with electronic sorcery.
He's out. Free.
He's heading home.
He takes three deep breaths and tries to ignore the tremor in his left hand.
A dozen confused SAMs, unable to find their target,
reach their apogee and fall helplessly toward Earth.
Plunging toward homes and schools and shops.
He stands on solid ground.
A cool wave of relief, breaks and washes over him.
Buddies return one by one.
His best friend is not among them.
That night he weeps quietly for a few minutes,
then falls into a troubled sleep.
Tomorrow he will fly again.
