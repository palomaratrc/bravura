South of Los Angeles, west of the desert
Linda Roux


I


Oranges smacking of amor y el mar, whose blossoms perfumed Scheherazade’s palms as she spread petals round her treacherous bed, that make mouths fructose-glad and dagger-tart,

require adversity to make them sweet: a brief coolness, but not the punishing cold that blackens green shoots and drags men from warm beds to trundle smudge pots down rows of stoic trees;

no, the right dose of pain can be justified when the sugar speaks up and the acid retorts; when the first bite of one elicits surprise like a flock of sun conures crossing the sky.

II


Driving, I’ve seen down a narrow side street the bluest ocean, a pagoda of clouds on top; and at night, attracted by the turquoise light of plankton proliferating in the liquid dark, I’ve seen their light smashed into pale blue atoms each time a wave broke itself on the sand; but now on this warm wood bench in the sun high above the sea it looks like someone has unrolled a bolt of peacock-blue silk shot through with some metallic thread and left it snapping in the wind from end to hemisphere-far end; today, in spite of family lost— one way or another, too many have left— the water is too wide and bright not to feel that even strafed it’s wonderful to be alive.

III

Top branches heave with marauding
crows

Possums keep counsel
with their ratlike tails,
waiting for the dark

It’s a good year for figs when they please the crows
though beetles big as bottle caps mount the fruit each June,
clasping it to bellies that iridesce deep-greenly,
with six pincer-legs sunk into the meat.
Fermenting figs eructate in the boozy heat.
A human comes by to cull rotten ones
and nudging a scarab in the canopy,
it drops like a rock to the leaf litter below
dead drunk or maybe oversated
and three more plumb bobs drop to their deaths,
victims of luscious excess.
Then half an hour later from under the loam,
wonderful to relate the little motors fly away;
their rubber bands had only needed rewinding

Ants concentrate
on long-term
fig investments

Roots allure pocket gophers
munchingly
