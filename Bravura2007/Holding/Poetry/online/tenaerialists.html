Ten aerialists
Linda Roux


We make our ways singly across the high wire,
each of the family venturing a step
into the broad blue delirium
above the striped tents and reeling rides.

This one advances in heartfelt increments,
practically level with the lunchtime sun,
then attempts a pirouette in a drastic wind;
there’s no hearing us once she’s begun that career.

Another’s dressed up in chartreuse, and shouts
her doubts while retreating down the ladder to earth,
and succeeds in pinning her frilly self there;
hopefully she hasn’t unnerved the rest.

Near her on the ground the simple one seeks
his slippers; and having never been able
to learn even rudimentary routines
perhaps he’s the most contented of all.

On the wire the strong-chested aerialist
navigates nearly to the far pedestal,
a gondolier plying the high, pelagic spaces
with an ill-balanced pole; he founders up there.

Two steps across and our silver-sequined sister
throws down her pole, then her body headfirst,
each spangle repeating the noon-high sun
as she falls fast and faster and farther beneath it.

For the rest of our troupe, it’s too soon to discern
the familiar flaw, or to know if we’ll have to
witness another botched acrobat.
Maybe the best way to reach the far end

is to stand on the platform when your turn comes,
let a breeze lift a few strands of your hair
and have a black laugh at our odds while leaning
lightly into that big blue vault.
