we women say 'watch your weight'
By Nicole Lee


typically freezing happens
when the temperature drops
but I am frozen now,
though the mercury’s
higher than any kite beneath
blizzard clouds; my herstory
compels me to save my energy
when the world tries to warm me,
‘cause you can never trust what’s
willing to cook you when it cooks
you for its own consumption

so I freeze instead of thaw.

I’m not meat you can tenderize
with whispered softened words,
cause silk makes rope as surely as
cotton and that I do realize

You set me in your sink and
turn on the faucet, thinking
water is a great conductor of
energy and will quickly relieve
me of my ice, warm my frosted
muscles. Are you surprised I
sit with a perfect crystallized
gaze and freeze the water that
falls on me, my own way to
conduct energy?

I am ripe when I am raw.
without homegrown blends
of rosemary and thyme,
I have flavor, sweet ache,
unhesitating anger, and
something else to savor—
bitterness, hope, and the
source of what I gave her

Holding back cause I’ll never
be your main dish I preserve
myself like berries in jam
‘cept without the boiling and
excess sweetness

I calculate on frostbite
cause I’m tired of being
wrapped tight til you're ready
to cook and, for the record, I
do not mix well with butter
substitutes--I taste best with the
real thing and do not thin me with
water; I am not 'light' with twenty
percent less fat: fuck that. I'm heavy,
hardy, a real meal and you can
not digest me. I'll never be
I’ll never be
I’ll never again be
your main course
