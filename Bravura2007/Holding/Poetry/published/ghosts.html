“Ghosts and Glass”
John Savage


glass shatters—

when handled with shaking hands.
hands that have had too much to drink.
or not enough.

in kitchens everywhere—

shelves are lined with the ghosts of forgotten teacups,
plates, wineglasses.
things dropped and swept away
like ancient cities
or bread crumbs.

i remember them now—

as i’m sitting at my desk
eating oreo cookies,
wiping up spilt orange juice with toilet paper.

but i’m not really thinking about broken glass—

it’s more about how things are so easily forgotten.
how ghosts are so easily made,
so easily ignored.

but not even that, really—

it’s more about how we make ghosts of things we drop.
of how we don’t try to fix something
when it’s so easily swept away.
