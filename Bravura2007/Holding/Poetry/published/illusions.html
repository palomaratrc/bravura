Illusions
Editors’ Choice Winner, Poetry
By Sarah O’Nan


You almost had me going.
I found myself believing
that those rabbits
pulled out of your hat
were real.
But I guess I was tricked,
because they disappeared
when you left.
And the bird you turned into a
funny mouse that danced when I
clapped my hands?
It flew out my window yesterday
squeaking something about smoked gouda.
So now it's all back to illusions,
mind games and sleight of hand,
and it's up to me to continue the facade.
I pull the cape out and over your eyes
while I wave my wand and try to fool you
and the audience
into believing my apathy.
But tell me again of the magic words...
I knew them once
when you whispered into my ear
as you sawed me in half.
