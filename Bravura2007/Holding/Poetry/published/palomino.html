Palomino
By Kelly Callahan


She bit her lip as she worked.
Her fingers intertwined in gleaming strands of sunshine,
separating and straightening as strays
danced in Summer’s afternoon whispers.

Kneeling, she reached the end of her descent.
The last wisps fell from her fingertips,
rejoining the glittering others
for a fleeting moment of flawless beauty.

But the world cannot condone such perfection.
With a stomp and a swish,
the flaxen and gold burst into myriad pieces
and were cast into the wind
to settle once more in tranquil chaos.
