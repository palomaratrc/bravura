Standard of Living
Natasha Heckendorn


There’s a spider in the corner
of the shower.
She’s been there for months,
But I don’t give a damn.
It’s not worth the effort.

I ought to move.
Just pick up.
Get out.
Vacate.

Showering with spiders,
Sleeping with rats.
I wonder how long I can take it.

One day I’ll be free of this.
I’m going to eat thin toast
With water chestnut spread.
I’ll drink red wine and read by a fire.

One morning, my spider is gone.
Did she die,
Or just move up?
