Why Do I Bother With Caffeine?
By Donna Booher


While finding myself,
thinking of you,
I will order a latte.
Expensive and
heavy on the cream.
Or, maybe, just two tablespoons worth.
But hold the sugar.
Well, maybe, one lump.

I will sip it slowly,
On a cold morning, of a hot day.
After celebrating two birthdays in line,
Wondering why I asked for a shot
of espresso,
instead of hot chocolate.
When I know I hate
espresso.

But I’ve already paid my dues. For the latte.
And sadly, there are no refunds. On lattes.
So against my better judgment,
I’ll chug it down;
As if it was a shot of tequila.
Even though it’s clearly too early,
For tequila.
Damn espresso.

But all in all this is a perfect way,
To spend a morning,
When thinking
of you.
Getting up early; waiting in line,
Till noon.
Just to over pay
for a cup of ruined coffee.
